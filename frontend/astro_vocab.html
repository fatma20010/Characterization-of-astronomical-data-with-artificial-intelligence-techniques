Astronomical Vocabulary
ASTRONOMY:

Astronomy is the study of celestial objects and phenomena
beyond Earth's atmosphere, including stars, planets, galaxies, and cosmic events,
aimed at understanding the universe.

STARS:

A star is a luminous sphere of plasma
sustained by gravity, with the Sun being the nearest to Earth.
Other visible stars appear as stationary points of light due to their great distances.

QSO:

A quasar,short for quasi-stellar radio source,
is an extremely luminous and energetic active galactic nucleus-AGN- that is powered
by a supermassive black hole at the center of the -huge- galaxy.

BLACK HOLE:

A black hole is a region of spacetime
where gravity is so strong that nothing, including light and other electromagnetic waves,
has enough energy to escape it

GALAXY:

A galaxy is a massive collection of stars,
gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes
and are the building blocks of the universe.
