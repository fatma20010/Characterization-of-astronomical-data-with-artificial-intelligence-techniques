HOW WE CLASSIFY!

In space, astronauts rely on a combination of position and brightness information to classify celestial objects. Right Ascension (RA) and Declination (Dec) pinpoint an object's location on the celestial sphere, similar to latitude and longitude on Earth. Magnitudes (u, g, r, i, z) measured through different colored filters reveal an object's overall brightness and spectral characteristics. By analyzing these values, astronauts can make educated guesses about an object's nature. For instance, extended objects with faint overall brightness (high magnitude values) and no significant variation across color filters might be galaxies. Conversely, a single point-like source with significant brightness (low magnitude values) across most filters could be a star.
